Western Water - Introduction

Watern Water does not include water from desalination plants, ground water, or water pulled directly from rivers Instead it focuses on water stored in reservoirs.

Data does not include every reservoir in California. Data for some reservoirs was not available for various reasons; mainly due to an inability to scrape data or determine a reservoir's capacity. Nor is water from Colorado river reservoirs, which provides water to much of southern California, as it's difficult to quantify how much water in each reservoir: Havasu, Mead and Powell belongs to California. The Colorado River Compact guarantees California 4,000,000 acre feet a year. So you can spread it among the Colorado River reservoirs however you see fit. If there is a shortfall in available water in the river California's water rights trump Arizona's.

Texas reservoir data should be complete. A handful of reservoirs in Texas are shared with other states or countries, with the water split as a percentage between the agreement partners.
